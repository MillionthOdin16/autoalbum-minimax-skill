# Producer and Mix Fingerprint Taxonomy

This reference helps translate producer, scene, and mix-style requests into MiniMax controls.

## Rhythmic fingerprint
- kick placement
- snare/clap weight
- hat density
- swing/shuffle
- percussion identity
- use of silence/dropouts

## Low-end fingerprint
- 808 glide vs live bass vs synth bass vs log drum
- sub length: short/punchy, long/sustained, sliding, pulsing
- relationship to kick
- low-end role: melodic, percussive, grounding, aggressive

## Harmonic fingerprint
- simple loop vs progression
- diatonic vs chromatic/borrowed chords
- extended harmony
- modal color
- cadence style

## Vocal-production fingerprint
- dry close vocal vs reverb-washed vocal
- pitch correction transparency
- doubles, stacks, gang vocals, ad-libs
- vocal position in mix
- intimacy vs spectacle

## Arrangement fingerprint
- intro length
- early hook vs delayed payoff
- pre-chorus lift
- bridge contrast
- breakdown/build/drop behavior
- outro/transition behavior

## Mix-space fingerprint
- dry / intimate
- wide / cinematic
- dark / low-passed
- bright / radio-polished
- lo-fi / clipped
- club / sub-forward
- live-room / organic

## MiniMax prompt application
When a user names a producer or scene, convert it into 3-5 fingerprint controls rather than only a name. The prompt should prioritize the controls most likely to affect generation: drums, bass, vocal posture, arrangement, and mix space.
