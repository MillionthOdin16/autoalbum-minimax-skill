# Detailed Latin and Global Fingerprints

## Modern reggaeton
- **Tempo / feel:** 85-100 BPM; dembow/tresillo groove, sensual forward motion.
- **Drums:** dembow kick/snare pattern, tight percussion, claps, syncopated hats.
- **Bass:** rounded sub or synth bass supporting dembow bounce.
- **Harmony:** minor/major loops, guitar or synth motifs, simple but sticky.
- **Vocals:** rhythmic singing/rap, confident phrasing, call-response hooks.
- **Hook mechanics:** chantable chorus, post-chorus melodic tag, rhythm-first phrasing.
- **Arrangement:** intro hook quickly, verse/hook alternation, percussive breakdown or bridge.
- **Mix:** clean vocal, percussion crisp, low end controlled.
- **Failure modes:** generic Latin pop without dembow, overly busy melody, weak percussion.

## Dominican dembow
- **Tempo / feel:** faster, rawer, high-energy dembow; often 105-130+ BPM depending route.
- **Drums:** relentless dembow percussion, sharp snares/claps, repetitive energy.
- **Bass:** simple, aggressive, supports rhythm more than harmony.
- **Harmony:** minimal; rhythm, vocal cadence, and attitude dominate.
- **Vocals:** shouted/commanding, playful, street-party energy.
- **Hook mechanics:** repeated phrase or chant, immediate crowd response.
- **Arrangement:** short loops, drops, breaks, high repetition.
- **Failure modes:** polished reggaeton instead of dembow, too melodic, not enough percussion drive.

## Afrobeats / Afropop
- **Tempo / feel:** 95-115 BPM; relaxed but danceable pocket.
- **Drums:** syncopated percussion, shakers, log/wood textures, kick patterns with bounce.
- **Bass:** warm rhythmic bass, often interacting with percussion.
- **Harmony:** bright major/minor loops, guitar/keys motifs.
- **Vocals:** melodic, laid-back, rhythmic; call-response and stacked hooks.
- **Hook mechanics:** repeated melodic phrase, danceable cadence, effortless feel.
- **Arrangement:** groove-first, variations through percussion/vocal layers.
- **Mix:** warm, open, midrange clarity, light reverb.
- **Failure modes:** generic tropical pop, stiff drums, overcomplicated lyrics.

## Amapiano
- **Tempo / feel:** 105-115 BPM; log drum bounce, spacious and hypnotic.
- **Drums:** shakers, soft kicks, percussive piano/keys, sparse but rolling groove.
- **Bass:** log drum is central identity; deep, percussive, syncopated.
- **Harmony:** piano chords, airy pads, jazz/house influence.
- **Vocals:** chants, short melodic phrases, group responses; instrumental space matters.
- **Hook mechanics:** log drum groove + vocal tag + piano motif.
- **Arrangement:** slow layering, drop/re-entry, dance-floor patience.
- **Mix:** deep but not overcrowded, percussive clarity.
- **Failure modes:** house without log drum, too much lyric density, weak groove patience.

## Dancehall
- **Tempo / feel:** 90-110 BPM or faster depending era; riddim-driven, syncopated.
- **Drums:** dancehall riddim patterns, offbeat accents, sharp snares.
- **Bass:** repetitive, physical, often simple but central.
- **Harmony:** minimal loops or bright chords.
- **Vocals:** patois-influenced cadence when appropriate, toasting/singjay energy, confident hooks.
- **Hook mechanics:** rhythmic phrase, callout, dance instruction or attitude phrase.
- **Arrangement:** riddim consistency with vocal contrast.
- **Failure modes:** generic reggae, no riddim specificity, too smooth.
