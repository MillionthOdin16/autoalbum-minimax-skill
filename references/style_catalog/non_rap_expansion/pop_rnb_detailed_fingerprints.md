# Detailed Pop and R&B Fingerprints

## Dark minimalist pop
- **Tempo / feel:** 70-105 BPM or half-time pop pulse; restrained, negative space, intimate.
- **Drums:** sparse kick/snare, finger snaps, sub drops, muted electronic percussion; avoid busy EDM drums.
- **Bass:** controlled sub, often entering after the vocal has established the emotional world.
- **Harmony:** minor or modal color, simple progressions, tension through restraint rather than complexity.
- **Vocals:** close-mic, breath, whisper-to-belt contrast, doubled hook whispers, layered background pads.
- **Hook mechanics:** short, severe title phrase; memorable through contour and space, not word count.
- **Arrangement:** verse is intimate and dry; chorus widens with harmonies/sub, not necessarily huge drums.
- **Mix:** dry lead, dark low end, filtered textures, ASMR-level vocal proximity.
- **Failure modes:** generic sad pop, overproduced dance chorus, too many metaphors, no hook lift.
- **MiniMax capsule:** "A dark minimalist pop song with close breathy vocals, sparse electronic percussion, controlled sub bass, and a chorus that widens through harmonies rather than a big drop."

## Contemporary radio pop
- **Tempo / feel:** 95-128 BPM, clean pulse, instantly legible structure.
- **Drums:** polished kick/snare or clap, tight transient control, lift into chorus.
- **Bass:** simple, supportive, hook-friendly; do not overcrowd lead vocal.
- **Harmony:** diatonic progressions with occasional color chords; pre-chorus tension is key.
- **Vocals:** highly centered lead, layered doubles, harmonies and ad-libs after first chorus.
- **Hook mechanics:** title phrase appears naturally; chorus can be remembered after one listen.
- **Arrangement:** short intro, early vocal, verse/pre/chorus clarity, post-chorus if earworm needed.
- **Mix:** bright but not harsh, polished top end, clean vocal presence.
- **Failure modes:** anonymous sync-pop, too many sections, chorus not bigger than verse.

## Alternative R&B
- **Tempo / feel:** 65-100 BPM; pocket, swing, nocturnal intimacy.
- **Drums:** soft trap hats, swung snares, rim clicks, sparse percussion, subtle groove changes.
- **Bass:** warm sub or rounded electric bass; often glides around vocal pockets.
- **Harmony:** extended chords, 7ths/9ths/11ths, borrowed color, unresolved cadences.
- **Vocals:** melismatic but understated, stacked harmonies, ad-libs as emotional shading.
- **Hook mechanics:** often melodic phrase or mood phrase rather than chant; sensual repetition.
- **Arrangement:** textural evolution, vocal layering, bridge with harmonic color or rhythmic drop-out.
- **Mix:** warm, hazy, wide pads, intimate vocal, low-mid care.
- **Failure modes:** generic trap-soul loop, mushy low mids, overlong intro, hook too subtle.

## Neo-soul / modern soul
- **Tempo / feel:** 70-100 BPM; live pocket, swing, human groove.
- **Drums:** live or live-feeling kit, ghost notes, rim shots, brushed/snapped textures.
- **Bass:** melodic electric bass, upright or warm synth bass, call-response with chords.
- **Harmony:** rich extensions, secondary dominants, chromatic passing chords, gospel/jazz colors.
- **Vocals:** expressive phrasing, runs with purpose, background choir stacks.
- **Hook mechanics:** lyrical/emotional payoff often more important than maximal simplicity.
- **Arrangement:** keys/guitar/bass interplay; bridge should change harmony meaningfully.
- **Mix:** warm analog-feeling, vocal naturalism, instruments breathe.
- **Failure modes:** lounge pastiche, over-complex chords with no song center, indulgent runs.

## Trap-soul / melodic R&B-rap crossover
- **Tempo / feel:** 65-90 BPM, often triplet or half-time.
- **Drums:** trap hats, sparse snare/clap, 808 glides, nocturnal pocket.
- **Bass:** 808 follows vocal emotion; can be melodic but should not dominate lead.
- **Harmony:** minor piano/guitar/synth loop, emotional simplicity, occasional suspended color.
- **Vocals:** sung-rap, Auto-Tuned melodic slides, intimate ad-libs.
- **Hook mechanics:** repeated confession phrase, melodic loop, easy emotional entry.
- **Arrangement:** verse/hook blur possible; keep enough structure for MiniMax tags.
- **Mix:** vocal upfront, dark reverb, airy doubles, sub clean.
- **Failure modes:** generic sad trap, too much lyrical abstraction, monotone loop.
