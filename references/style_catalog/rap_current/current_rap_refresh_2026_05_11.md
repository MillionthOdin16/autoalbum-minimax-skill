## High-priority current lanes

### Don Toliver — E85 / nocturnal luxury melodic trap
- **Core:** elastic melodic vocals, heavy low-end, glossy night-drive synths, dark romance, expensive haze.
- **Tempo:** 70-90 BPM trap ballad feel or 120-150 double-time perception.
- **Drums:** polished trap grid, rolling hats, wide snare/clap, bass-forward but not rage-distorted.
- **Vocal:** slippery, melodic, compressed, floating over the pocket; less conversational than Drake and less psychedelic/ad-lib-drenched than Travis.
- **Hook:** vowel-rich melodic phrase that can survive repetition.
- **Prompt capsule:** A nocturnal melodic trap track with glossy synth haze, heavy rounded 808s, elastic male vocals, and a dark-romantic hook that feels expensive and wounded.
- **Do not confuse with:** Travis Scott atmosphere-rocking festival scale; Drake caption-bar confessional; Future narcotic monotone menace.

### BossMan Dlow — Florida motion / money-talk bounce
- **Core:** bright, bouncy, repetitive flex rap with quotable motion/business phrases and simple club-pocket drums.
- **Tempo:** 90-105 BPM bounce or double-time trap feel.
- **Drums:** crisp southern drums, clean clap/snare, bouncy hats, short 808 punch.
- **Vocal:** direct, nasal/bright, conversational command; ad-libs and punch-ins should feel casually arrogant.
- **Hook:** short repetitive money/motion phrase, easy to chant.
- **Prompt capsule:** A bright Florida motion rap track with bouncy southern drums, short punchy 808s, crisp claps, and direct confident vocals built around a repetitive money-talk hook.
- **Do not confuse with:** GloRilla's Memphis stomp, Sexyy Red's raunchy chant minimalism, or Lil Baby's breathless melodic street flow.

### Pooh Shiesty — modern Memphis street menace
- **Core:** cold Memphis street rap, sparse ominous keys, clipped flow, controlled threat.
- **Tempo:** 80-100 BPM, often dark half-time.
- **Drums:** sparse trap drums, hard snare, little wasted motion, menacing 808 punctuation.
- **Vocal:** dry, low-emotion, precise, blunt; menace comes from restraint rather than yelling.
- **Hook:** grim phrase, street logic, low-melody repetition.
- **Prompt capsule:** A cold modern Memphis street-rap track with sparse ominous piano, clipped 808 hits, hard dry snares, and controlled low-emotion vocals that sound threatening without shouting.
- **Do not confuse with:** Juicy J crunk-party chant, Future melodic toxicity, or drill's sliding-808 swing.

### DaBaby — bounce-rap comeback lane
- **Core:** energetic, comedic/aggressive bounce rap with hard pocket, punchy delivery, and quick cadence changes.
- **Tempo:** 90-110 BPM bounce; percussion must leave space for vocal athleticism.
- **Drums:** bouncy kicks, sharp claps, minimal melodic loop, forward vocal mix.
- **Vocal:** loud, animated, percussive, confident; flow locks tightly to drum accents.
- **Hook:** short chantable phrase with personality, often delivered like a punchline.
- **Prompt capsule:** An energetic bounce-rap track with hard southern drums, a minimal loop, loud animated vocals, fast pocket changes, and a short punchline hook built for replay.
- **Do not confuse with:** Lil Baby melodic flow, Kendrick character acting, or BossMan Dlow's lazier money-talk bounce.

### Hunxho — romantic pain-rap / street R&B hybrid
- **Core:** emotional street melodies, romantic regret, soft-trap drums, warm piano/guitar, melodic pain delivery.
- **Tempo:** 70-95 BPM.
- **Drums:** trap-soul pocket, soft snare/clap, warm 808.
- **Vocal:** melodic, wounded, direct; less pop-polished than Drake, less posthumous emo-rock than Juice WRLD.
- **Hook:** repeated confession or apology line; simple enough to sing.
- **Prompt capsule:** A romantic pain-rap track with warm piano or guitar, soft trap drums, rounded 808s, and wounded melodic vocals repeating a direct confession in the chorus.
- **Do not confuse with:** Juice WRLD emo melodrama, Rod Wave gospel-belt pain, or Drake luxury-confessional polish.
