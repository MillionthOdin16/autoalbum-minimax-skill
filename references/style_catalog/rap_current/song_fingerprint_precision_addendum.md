# Current Rap Song Fingerprint Precision Addendum

Use this file when the user names a specific song or asks for a track to sit in a very specific lane.

## Song-reference analysis fields
Each song reference must be reduced into:
- BPM/tempo feel,
- rhythmic grid,
- drum identity,
- bass/808 identity,
- loop/sample/synth identity,
- vocal entrance,
- hook mechanism,
- verse density,
- arrangement turns,
- mix space,
- replay engine,
- what not to copy from adjacent hits.

## Current high-value song lanes

### Kendrick Lamar & SZA — `luther` lane
- **Use for:** romantic rap/R&B crossover with emotional polish and duet gravity.
- **Controls:** warm soul/R&B bed, elegant melodic chorus, restrained drums, romantic thesis, high emotional clarity.
- **Do not collapse into:** generic Drake R&B; keep Kendrick-style moral/poetic specificity and SZA-like lushness if using duet energy.

### Kendrick Lamar — `Not Like Us` / West Coast chant-diss lane
- **Use for:** competitive anthem, crowd chant, cultural-region identity.
- **Controls:** West Coast bounce, crisp claps, dry vocal, short slogan hook, taunting phrasing, DJ-drop energy.
- **Do not collapse into:** generic trap diss; keep bounce and chant mechanics.

### Playboi Carti — `Evil J0rdan` / MUSIC-era Opium lane
- **Use for:** maximal punk-trap, moshpit energy, voice/persona as instrument.
- **Controls:** distorted bass, abrasive digital synths, repetitive chant, ad-libs, sudden vocal textures.
- **Do not collapse into:** Yeat bell-rage or Ken Carson one-note distortion; keep Carti's space/persona/ad-lib ecology.

### Don Toliver — `E85` lane
- **Use for:** current melodic trap with dark luxury and smooth replay value.
- **Controls:** nocturnal synths, heavy rounded bass, elastic vocal hook, romantic tension, polished trap drums.
- **Do not collapse into:** Travis cavernous psychedelia or Drake conversational R&B.

### BossMan Dlow — `Motion Party` lane
- **Use for:** current party/motion/money-talk rap.
- **Controls:** bouncy southern drums, short phrase hook, casually arrogant vocal, bright club motion, simple memorable cadence.
- **Do not collapse into:** Sexyy Red raunch chant or GloRilla Memphis stomp.

### Pooh Shiesty — `FDO` lane
- **Use for:** cold street menace and sparse modern Memphis gravity.
- **Controls:** ominous loop, clipped vocal, hard snare, short 808 punctuation, low-emotion threat.
- **Do not collapse into:** crunk chant or melodic trap.

### DaBaby — `POP DAT THANG` lane
- **Use for:** high-energy bounce, animated hook, party aggression.
- **Controls:** tight bouncy drums, loud animated voice, punchline cadence, minimal loop, short chant hook.
- **Do not collapse into:** Lil Baby urgency or BossMan Dlow lazy motion.

## Song-lane prompt rule
A song-lane prompt should mention the reference anchor and then spend most of its words on audible traits. Do not add five unrelated artist references.
