# Current Rap Artist Fingerprints — High-Detail MiniMax Catalog

Use these profiles when the user requests a specific current rapper, a close artist lane, or an album with modern rap fingerprints. Each profile is designed to compile into MiniMax Music 2.6 prompts and should be used with `style_strictness` 4–5 for artist-specific requests.

Important: do not blur adjacent artists. The `Differentiators` section exists to prevent generic trap output.

## Kendrick Lamar — 2024–2026 GNX / West Coast dominance lane

**Current lane:** West Coast competitive rap, cinematic concept rap, chant-hook bangers, soul-sample love songs, and hyper-controlled vocal character acting.

**Why this profile matters:** Dominant 2025 chart presence via `luther`, `Not Like Us`, `tv off`, `squabble up`, and GNX-era visibility. Use for serious albums needing lyrical authority, regional identity, and concept-level cohesion.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 84–105 BPM for bounce/anthem tracks; 70–90 BPM for narrative cuts; can feel double-time. Strong use of swing, left-pocket phrasing, and sectional beat changes.
- **Drums / groove:** Mustard-adjacent West Coast bounce, crisp claps, DJ-style drops, percussive vocal chops, occasional G-funk or hyphy lift. Drums should feel dry, bright, and physically danceable, not cloudy trap mush.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Sub/808 often short and punctuating rather than constantly smeared; bassline can behave like West Coast funk movement or clipped trap knock.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Minor-mode West Coast synth motifs, chopped soul samples, sparse piano, gospel/soul warmth for emotional songs, and occasional eerie minimal loops for diss/competitive tracks.
- **Vocal delivery:** Highly articulated, theatrical, agile; switches between conversational, nasal taunt, shouted chant, preacher cadence, clipped whisper, and machine-gun internal rhyme. Vocal character changes are a primary signature.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks often work as chants, taunts, slogans, or elegantly simple emotional thesis lines. Strong call-response potential. Repetition should feel weaponized, not lazy.
- **Lyric posture:** Dense but legible. Themes: moral accounting, self-interrogation, Compton/L.A. pride, rivalry, fatherhood, spiritual pressure, industry critique, and victory lap. Uses compressed images and punchline turns, not generic flexing.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Can move from sparse verse bed to communal chant chorus; often uses interludes, voice snippets, abrupt switches, or guest texture to make the track feel like a scene.
- **Mix / texture:** Dry forward vocal; percussion sharply separated; samples and synths should leave room for bars. Loud, assertive, and clean enough for every syllable to land.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A sharp West Coast rap track with dry, commanding lead vocals, crisp claps, clipped sub bass, and a chant-like hook built for crowd response. The beat should feel like L.A. street bounce with cinematic tension: sparse synth stabs, sudden DJ-style drops, and a chorus that turns the central phrase into a slogan. The vocal performance should switch characters between calm precision, taunting emphasis, and urgent double-time pockets.

### Differentiators
Not Drake: less crooned/vulnerable and more confrontational/moral. Not J. Cole: more theatrical and rhythmically volatile. Not Travis: vocal front-and-center rather than atmosphere-led.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not make this generic boom-bap, sleepy conscious rap, or cloud trap. Avoid overlong metaphors that kill the hook. Avoid buried vocals. Avoid smooth R&B drums unless specifically targeting `luther` lane.

## Drake — modern melodic rap / Toronto luxury-confessional lane

**Current lane:** Glossy rap/R&B crossover, melodic hooks, quotable caption bars, icy Toronto night-drive atmosphere, and club-ready rap records.

**Why this profile matters:** One of the highest-scale streaming rappers; a primary reference for commercial rap albums that need melody, lifestyle, emotional accessibility, and playlist reach.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 70–95 BPM for R&B/trap ballads; 95–115 BPM for rap bounce; 120–135 for dance/club hybrids. Pocket should feel relaxed, conversational, and effortless.
- **Drums / groove:** Sparse trap hats, soft kicks, atmospheric snares, dancehall/house/Afrobeats touches when needed, and polished pop-rap drums that never overcrowd the voice.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Rounded 808s, warm sub-bass, or soft club bass. Bass should be smooth and luxurious rather than aggressive unless doing a rap-first diss lane.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Minor-key piano loops, icy pads, filtered samples, moody synth plucks, chopped vocal samples, and high-gloss R&B chord extensions.
- **Vocal delivery:** Half-rapped half-sung, conversational, intimate, sometimes deadpan. Smooth vowel-heavy melody, low-stress rhythmic confidence, occasional sharper bar-heavy verse.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks are caption-ready and repeatable; often a simple phrase with melodic contour and emotional ambiguity. Strong pre-chorus or refrain can carry the song more than a traditional chorus.
- **Lyric posture:** Romantic grievance, fame fatigue, luxury isolation, loyalty tests, petty comedy, status anxiety, nightlife, soft regret, and confident dominance. Lines should feel quotable and diaristic.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Verse-hook-verse-hook with polished bridges or beat switches. Often alternates sung emotional pockets and rap flex pockets.
- **Mix / texture:** Clean, expensive, spacious; vocal very present with glossy compression and reverb/delay tails. Low end controlled and radio-ready.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A glossy Toronto melodic rap track with intimate half-sung vocals, icy minor-key piano, soft trap drums, and warm sub bass. The song should feel like a late-night confession from a luxury apartment after a club night, balancing romance, status, resentment, and dry humor. Keep the hook simple, quotable, and melodically sticky, with verses that glide between rap cadence and understated singing.

### Differentiators
Not Future: less slurred/toxic and more conversational. Not Don Toliver: less psychedelic and more diaristic. Not Travis: less stadium atmosphere, more human narration.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not overdo Auto-Tune into emo trap. Do not make the drums too cheap or aggressive. Avoid generic sad-boy clichés; the writing needs status detail, social observation, and quotable specificity.

## Playboi Carti — MUSIC / Opium rage-chaos lane

**Current lane:** Rage, experimental Atlanta trap, punk-adjacent chant rap, blown-out bass, distorted synth worlds, ad-lib architecture, and persona-first minimal lyricism.

**Why this profile matters:** Carti's `MUSIC` became a major 2025 rap event, with every song charting and the album debuting at No. 1. Use for high-energy albums, rage cuts, fashion-chaos tracks, mosh-pit hooks, and alien vocal identity.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 130–165 BPM rage feel, often half-time vocal pocket. Can also use slower 70–90 BPM trap with chaotic ad-lib movement.
- **Drums / groove:** Distorted trap drums, hard claps, rapid hats, punk-like rhythmic urgency, occasional EDM/rave pulse. Drums should feel violent, repetitive, and hypnotic.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Aggressive 808s, clipped distortion, blown-out sub drops, often more impact than melodic movement.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Serrated synth leads, horror pads, video-game/plastic textures, organ/choir stabs, synthetic guitar walls, and intentionally abrasive timbres.
- **Vocal delivery:** Voice is an instrument: baby voice, deep voice, raspy chant, shouted fragments, whispered asides, vamping, ad-libs as counter-rhythm. Semantic density is less important than texture and swagger.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks can be one phrase, repeated until it becomes physical. Catchiness comes from phonetics, ad-libs, and rhythmic placement, not narrative development.
- **Lyric posture:** Fashion, drugs, danger, chaos, vamp/goth imagery, superstar detachment, club violence, alien confidence. Lyrics are sparse and iconic rather than explanatory.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Loop-based, high-repetition, with beat drops and texture shifts. A long intro can work if it builds menace, but avoid dead air.
- **Mix / texture:** Loud, distorted, bass-forward, intentionally abrasive but not muddy. Vocal can sit inside the chaos rather than above it.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A distorted rage-trap track with blown-out 808s, serrated synth leads, hard claps, and an alien vocal performance that switches between raspy chants, deep-voice flexes, and sharp ad-libs. The hook should be extremely repetitive and physical, designed for a mosh pit rather than lyrical explanation. Keep the atmosphere dark, synthetic, fashionable, and chaotic.

### Differentiators
Not Travis: less cinematic and more raw/repetitive. Not Yeat: less robotic language-world, more punk vamp swagger. Not Future: less emotional melody, more persona texture.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not make this standard trap with normal verses. Do not make lyrics too coherent or verbose. Do not remove distortion. Avoid polished pop choruses unless doing `Rather Lie` / crossover Carti.

## Travis Scott — psychedelic stadium trap lane

**Current lane:** Atmospheric trap, arena-scale hooks, psychedelic synth design, Auto-Tuned vocal layering, beat switches, and cinematic feature-driven arrangements.

**Why this profile matters:** A top streaming rap artist and a standard reference for modern atmospheric rap production. Use when the album needs scale, worldbuilding, festival energy, or immersive production.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 70–85 BPM half-time trap or 130–150 double-time; can include house/club elements. Groove is often hypnotic and spacious.
- **Drums / groove:** Heavy trap drums, booming kicks, wide snares, rolling hats, abrupt beat switches, and large-scale breakdowns. Drums support spectacle.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Huge 808s, long sub sustain, bass drops that feel architectural, sometimes distorted but controlled.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Dark synth pads, psychedelic arps, choir/gospel fragments, distorted guitars, electronic textures, filtered samples, and sci-fi atmospheres.
- **Vocal delivery:** Auto-Tuned, ad-lib-heavy, often more atmospheric than lyrical. Uses elongated vowels, stacked harmonies, echo responses, and shouted crowd-hype phrases.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks often ride a simple melodic loop with chantable fragments. The song's hook may be production-driven as much as lyric-driven.
- **Lyric posture:** Fame, escape, night drives, intoxication, spectacle, surreal luxury, emotional numbness. Less narrative specificity; more sensory atmosphere.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Intro atmosphere -> main trap section -> beat switch/breakdown -> feature/vocal stack -> outro texture. Contrast and transitions are key.
- **Mix / texture:** Wide stereo, reverb-heavy, dark and expensive. Vocal often blended into the production, with ad-libs flying around the stereo field.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A dark psychedelic stadium-trap track with huge 808s, wide snares, hypnotic synth pads, and Auto-Tuned vocals layered with echoing ad-libs. The song should feel like a midnight festival set inside a futuristic city, with a spacious intro, heavy trap drop, and a dramatic beat switch that opens into a wider second half.

### Differentiators
Not Drake: less conversational and more world/atmosphere. Not Carti: more cinematic and structured. Not Future: less bluesy pain, more spectacle.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not make the vocal too dry or lyric-forward. Do not flatten the arrangement into one loop. Avoid generic trap without a distinct sonic environment.

## Future — toxic melodic trap / Pluto lane

**Current lane:** Auto-Tuned trap-singing, melancholy flexing, toxic romance, narcotic atmosphere, and emotionally smeared street luxury.

**Why this profile matters:** One of trap's most influential living templates; remains a top streaming rapper and a core reference for melodic trap, dark trap, and Metro Boomin-style album work.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 65–85 BPM half-time trap; 120–150 double-time feel. Pocket should feel slurred, behind-the-beat, and emotionally heavy.
- **Drums / groove:** Atlanta trap drums: rolling hats, cold claps, snare snaps, open-hat lifts, occasional triumphant trap brass or dark Metro bounce.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Sustained 808 slides, ominous sub movement, heavy but melodic bassline pressure.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Minor piano, gothic bells, eerie pads, cinematic strings, dark guitar loops, and expensive synthetic melancholy.
- **Vocal delivery:** Mumbled/melodic Auto-Tune, emotionally blurred, sometimes shouted but still slurry. Melodies should feel improvised and wounded, not polished pop.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks are mantra-like and emotionally contradictory: flex as confession, toxicity as blues, one repeated phrase becoming mood hypnosis.
- **Lyric posture:** Drugs, betrayal, women, success, numbness, paranoia, luxury, regret, street codes. The emotional center is damaged abundance.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Loop-driven, hook-first, with verse flows that feel like spilling thoughts. Bridges are rare; beat drops and ad-lib/vocal layering create movement.
- **Mix / texture:** Dark, bass-heavy, Auto-Tune forward but not clean-pop. Reverb/delay smear around the vocal; hi-hats crisp over murky low end.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A dark Atlanta melodic trap song with heavy sliding 808s, rolling hi-hats, minor piano, and narcotic Auto-Tuned vocals. The performance should feel toxic, wounded, and luxurious at once: a mantra-like hook, slurred melodic verses, and ad-libs that trail like smoke. Keep the mood nocturnal, expensive, paranoid, and emotionally numb.

### Differentiators
Not Drake: more slurred and instinctive. Not Gunna: darker and more emotionally corroded. Not Don Toliver: less psychedelic R&B, more trap blues.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not over-articulate the vocal. Do not make the lyrics too healthy or moralistic. Avoid cheerful major-key trap unless intentionally contrasting.

## Metro Boomin — cinematic dark trap producer lane

**Current lane:** Producer fingerprint: ominous minor loops, spacious drums, iconic drops, villainous atmosphere, and artist-specific beat architecture.

**Why this profile matters:** Metro remains one of the most important producers in current rap; his sound anchors Future, 21 Savage, Travis Scott, The Weeknd/Carti crossover, and superhero-cinematic rap worlds.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** Usually 65–85 BPM half-time or 130–160 double-time. Groove is spacious and exact, with negative space for vocal authority.
- **Drums / groove:** Sparse but massive drums: hard snare/clap, crisp hats, dramatic open-hat entries, dropouts, and producer-tag-like moments.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Deep 808 with long sustain, slides, or suspenseful sub stabs. Bass enters as an event.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Minor bells, piano, choir, strings, horror synths, reversed textures, brass stabs, and cinematic pads. Motifs should be simple and memorable.
- **Vocal delivery:** Depends on artist, but production should leave room for low, deadpan, melodic, or stadium vocals.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hook often appears after an ominous setup. The beat itself should have a recognizable motif that functions as a hook.
- **Lyric posture:** Villainy, distrust, luxury danger, street mythology, betrayal, night, cars, power, and cinematic tension.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Strong intro identity, sudden beat drop, verse/chorus spacing, mid-song beat switch or stripped bridge if needed.
- **Mix / texture:** Huge lows, clean top end, dark center. Leave dramatic silence and dropouts.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A cinematic dark trap track with ominous minor piano, horror-bell accents, deep sustained 808s, crisp hi-hats, and a dramatic drop after a suspenseful intro. The production should feel villainous and expensive, with enough negative space for a dominant rapper and a hook built from a simple, haunting motif.

### Differentiators
Not Southside: less maximal aggression, more cinematic control. Not Mustard: less bounce, more darkness. Not Travis-only: producer identity comes from the beat motif, not only atmosphere.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not overfill the arrangement. Do not make the melody too complex. Avoid bright pop synths unless intentionally doing crossover.

## Lil Baby — Atlanta motivational melodic street-rap lane

**Current lane:** Rapid melodic street rap, triumphant minor-key beats, hustle testimony, and tightly packed flows that still feel radio-ready.

**Why this profile matters:** A core modern Atlanta rap reference. Use when the album needs street credibility, forward momentum, and motivational urgency.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 110–135 BPM melodic trap range; often double-time rap over half-time drums. Flow should accelerate and tumble through pockets.
- **Drums / groove:** Trap drums with bright hats, tight snares, frequent rolls, and forward-driving bounce. Drums should feel expensive but urgent.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Clean 808s with slides; supports flow without overpowering it.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Epic pianos, orchestral pads, guitar loops, arpeggiated keys, dark synth pads, often in A minor, D minor, or F minor-type moods.
- **Vocal delivery:** Half-sung half-rapped but less slurred than Future; rapid, nasal, compressed, and relentless. Flow switches are important.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks are melodic but still rapped; often short motivational phrases or street-life refrains that can be repeated without sounding pop-soft.
- **Lyric posture:** Hustle, survival, loyalty, betrayal, wealth earned through struggle, responsibility, street memories, upward mobility.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Verse-heavy with hook anchors; bridges uncommon. Movement comes from flow escalation and beat intensity.
- **Mix / texture:** Bright trap mix, vocal dry and upfront, melody loop clear, hats crisp. Less murky than Future; less glossy than Drake.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A motivational Atlanta melodic-trap track around 120–130 BPM with rapid half-sung rap flows, bright rolling hats, clean 808 slides, and an epic minor-key piano or guitar loop. The hook should feel street-rooted but anthemic, with verses that accelerate through hustle details, loyalty conflicts, and earned-success imagery.

### Differentiators
Not Gunna: more urgent and bar-packed. Not Future: clearer and less narcotic. Not Drake: less glossy and more street-testimony focused.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not make the flow too slow or crooned. Avoid generic trap flexing without narrative pressure. Do not bury the vocal.

## Gunna — polished drip-flow luxury trap lane

**Current lane:** Smooth melodic Atlanta trap, liquid flow, fashion/luxury imagery, atmospheric beats, and pocket-first cool.

**Why this profile matters:** Still a major streaming/reference artist for polished melodic trap. Useful for sleek album tracks, midtempo luxury, and vibe-forward songs.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 120–145 BPM trap, but vocal feels laid-back. Groove should glide rather than attack.
- **Drums / groove:** Clean trap drums, crisp hats, restrained bounce, little clutter. The beat must leave space for flow to skate.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Round 808s, smooth slides, not too aggressive. Bass supports fluidity.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Atmospheric pads, guitars, plucks, bells, watery synths, soft keys. Melodies often feel polished and weightless.
- **Vocal delivery:** Relaxed, fluid, low-friction melodic rap. Less emotional pain than Future; less urgent than Lil Baby. Pocket is everything.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks are vibe hooks: catchy by flow and texture, not huge pop choruses. Refrains should feel effortless.
- **Lyric posture:** Fashion, wealth, cars, women, travel, drip, composure, subtle paranoia. Imagery should be stylish and sensory.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Loop-driven, smooth transitions, minimal drama. Songs often succeed by hypnotic consistency.
- **Mix / texture:** Pristine beats, smooth vocal compression, glossy high end, controlled low end.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A polished Atlanta luxury-trap track with smooth melodic rap vocals, crisp hi-hats, rounded 808s, and an atmospheric guitar or pluck loop. The performance should glide effortlessly with fashion-forward imagery and relaxed confidence, using a hypnotic hook that feels catchy without shouting.

### Differentiators
Not Lil Baby: less urgent. Not Future: less damaged and darker. Not Travis: smaller and smoother, less cinematic.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not make it too aggressive or lyrically dense. Do not overdo the chorus. Avoid muddy low end; this lane needs clean polish.

## Don Toliver — psychedelic melodic rap/R&B lane

**Current lane:** Melodic trap-soul, warped vocal melodies, sensual night-drive atmosphere, and psychedelic Houston-adjacent production.

**Why this profile matters:** A current rap/R&B crossover anchor with strong feature presence and album utility for hooks, late-night records, and melodic texture.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 70–95 BPM for slow glide; 100–125 for danceable trap/R&B. Groove is swaying and narcotic.
- **Drums / groove:** Trap/R&B hybrid drums, soft snares, pocketed hats, sometimes bouncy club percussion.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Warm sub, rolling 808s, and bass movement that supports melody more than aggression.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Wavy synths, dark guitars, vaporous pads, retro-futurist keys, psychedelic R&B chords.
- **Vocal delivery:** Distinctive nasal melodic belt, glides, yelps, and sustained vowels. Vocal melody is the main instrument.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks should be smooth, vowel-rich, and instantly singable, often floating above the beat.
- **Lyric posture:** Romance, intoxication, desire, nightlife, fast cars, longing, sensual detachment, emotional blur.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Often intro-hook-verse-hook, with instrumental pockets and vocal stacks. The track should feel like motion at night.
- **Mix / texture:** Lush, wide, and saturated. Vocal wet but present. Bass warm, not punishing.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A psychedelic melodic rap/R&B track with warm sub bass, soft trap drums, wavy synth pads, and a distinctive nasal lead vocal carrying long vowel-heavy melodies. The song should feel like a late-night drive through neon haze, mixing desire, intoxication, and emotional distance with a smooth, floating hook.

### Differentiators
Not Drake: more melodic and psychedelic. Not Future: less toxic, more sensual. Not Travis: less stadium, more vocal-hook centered.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not make the vocal too plain. Avoid bar-heavy verses. Avoid dry mixes; this lane needs atmosphere.

## 21 Savage — deadpan menace / minimalist trap lane

**Current lane:** Cold deadpan delivery, stripped trap beats, dry threats, dark humor, and precise pocket control.

**Why this profile matters:** A staple modern rap voice and frequent top-tier collaborator. Use for menace, restraint, dry punchlines, and contrast against melodic artists.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 65–80 BPM half-time; 130–150 double-time. Pocket is slow, clipped, and controlled.
- **Drums / groove:** Minimal trap drums: hard snare/clap, sparse hats, cold kick placement, little swing.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Dark 808 hits, simple slides, threatening sub pressure.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Ominous piano, horror bells, minimal strings, dark pads, or skeletal loops.
- **Vocal delivery:** Flat, calm, emotionless, low-stress. Delivery should sound dangerous because it does not try hard.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks are blunt, simple, dry, and repetitive. The hook can be almost spoken.
- **Lyric posture:** Street violence, money, loyalty, distrust, legal trauma, dark humor, matter-of-fact flexing. Avoid emotional over-explanation.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Sparse loop, verse dominance, hook as mantra, occasional beat dropout for punchline emphasis.
- **Mix / texture:** Dry vocal, dark low end, minimal reverb. Space matters.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A cold minimalist trap track with ominous piano, sparse drums, hard 808 hits, and a deadpan low-register rap vocal. The hook should be blunt and repetitive, while the verses deliver dry menace, dark humor, and clipped punchlines over a skeletal beat with plenty of negative space.

### Differentiators
Not Future: no melodic smear. Not Lil Baby: less rapid/urgent. Not Metro-only: the vocal is dry and unbothered, not cinematic showboating.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not make the vocal emotional or sung. Avoid busy drums. Avoid cartoon villain energy; the menace should be understated.

## NBA YoungBoy — raw melodic pain/aggression lane

**Current lane:** Emotionally raw Southern trap, aggressive verses, melodic pain hooks, YouTube-era intensity, and unpolished urgency.

**Why this profile matters:** One of rap's strongest streaming cult artists. Use for pain rap, paranoia, trauma, loyalty conflict, and volatile vocal energy.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 70–95 BPM half-time trap; can feel fast through vocal intensity. Groove should leave room for emotional outbursts.
- **Drums / groove:** Southern trap drums, urgent hats, snare rolls, sometimes Louisiana bounce/throwback elements.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Heavy 808s but not overly polished. Bass should feel raw and direct.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Piano/guitar loops, mournful keys, minor melodies, sometimes throwback Louisiana rap motifs.
- **Vocal delivery:** Switches between strained melodic singing, yelling, and rapid aggressive rap. Voice should crack with intensity, not sound studio-perfect.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks are raw, direct, emotionally exposed. Repetition should sound like obsession or pain.
- **Lyric posture:** Trauma, loyalty, paranoia, violence, regret, family, isolation, betrayal, survival. The writing should feel immediate and unfiltered.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Verse-heavy, with hooks that feel like emotional eruptions. Abrupt tone shifts are acceptable.
- **Mix / texture:** Less polished than Drake/Gunna. Vocal close and intense; mix can be rougher, with urgency over luxury.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A raw Southern pain-rap track with mournful piano or guitar, heavy 808s, urgent trap drums, and a volatile vocal that switches from strained melodic hooks to aggressive rapid verses. The song should feel paranoid, loyal, wounded, and confrontational, with direct lyrics that sound like they were written in the middle of the crisis.

### Differentiators
Not Juice WRLD: less polished emo melody, more street volatility. Not Future: less narcotic luxury, more raw confession. Not Lil Baby: less motivational, more unstable.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not over-polish. Do not make lyrics abstract. Avoid clean pop choruses; the hook needs rawness.

## Doechii — theatrical alt-rap / precision-performance lane

**Current lane:** High-concept rap performance, theatrical character switches, funk/soul/jazz/club influences, technical control, and charismatic unpredictability.

**Why this profile matters:** One of the most visible current creative rap performers, valuable for albums needing theatricality, female rap agility, and left-field mainstream appeal.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 80–115 BPM depending on groove; can flip from conversational to rapid. Strong pocket changes.
- **Drums / groove:** Funk-informed drums, hip-hop breaks, club bounce, syncopated snares, and rhythm-section detail.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Basslines can be live/funky or synthetic and bouncy. Movement matters more than sub weight.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Funk guitar, jazz keys, soul chords, weird synths, and theatrical transitions.
- **Vocal delivery:** Hyper-articulated, animated, switches characters quickly; can rap, speak, chant, and sing with control.
- **Hook mechanics:** Hooks can be theatrical refrains, ironic slogans, or rhythmic chants. They should carry personality.
- **Lyric posture:** Self-mythology, wit, sexuality, vulnerability, social performance, mental pressure, high-status weirdness, surreal comedy.
- **Arrangement architecture:** Scene-like arrangement with skits, voice switches, stops, and changes in perspective. Can tolerate unusual structure if hook remains clear.
- **Mix / texture:** Vocal very forward; production colorful but not distracting. Dynamic range and contrast are key.

### MiniMax prompt capsule
A theatrical alt-rap track with crisp funk-influenced drums, a rubbery bassline, colorful synths, and an animated female rap vocal that changes characters between sharp technical bars, spoken asides, and a chantable hook. The song should feel witty, precise, slightly unhinged, and performance-driven, with at least one dramatic stop or switch.

### Differentiators
Not Nicki: less Barbie/gloss and more art-school theater. Not Cardi: less brash club simplicity, more technical shape-shifting. Not Kendrick: similar theatricality but more playful and feminine-coded performance.

### Avoid / failure modes
Do not make it generic female trap. Avoid flat delivery. Avoid overloading the beat so the performance loses control.

A Memphis club-rap track with a deep commanding female vocal, hard claps, punchy 808s, and a blunt chant hook built for crowd response. The beat should be simple, dark, and physical, leaving space for direct bars, funny threats, and short repeated slogans.

Not Sexyy Red: more commanding and less intentionally messy. Not Cardi: less glossy pop-trap, more Memphis grit. Not Doechii: less theatrical complexity, more club command.

## Sexyy Red — raw St. Louis club-rap / ratchet chant lane

**Current lane:** Unfiltered club rap, crude humor, minimalist beats, nasal directness, and meme-ready chant hooks.

**Why this profile matters:** A current cultural force in club rap and female rap. Useful for raw party tracks, viral hooks, and deliberately unpolished charisma.

### Style fingerprint
- **Tempo / feel:** 85–105 BPM club/trap bounce. Groove should be simple, sticky, and easy to dance to.
- **Drums / groove:** Minimal trap or club drums, loud claps, sparse hats, repetitive bounce. Nothing too ornate.
- **808 / bass behavior:** Simple 808 patterns; bass should hit but not become cinematic.
- **Harmony / melodic language:** Cheap-sounding synths, basic piano stabs, stripped loops, intentionally rough textures.
- **Vocal delivery:** Nasal, casual, blunt, almost conversational; confidence comes from not trying to sound polished.
