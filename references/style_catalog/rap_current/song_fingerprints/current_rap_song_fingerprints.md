# Current Popular Rap Song Fingerprints — MiniMax Reference Cards

These cards convert current/high-impact rap songs into generation-useful fingerprints. Use them when the user names a song reference, asks for a track to follow a specific hit lane, or wants a similar role inside an album.

Do not copy lyrics. Capture the musical function, groove, arrangement, vocal posture, hook mechanics, and production fingerprint.

## Kendrick Lamar & SZA — `luther` lane

**Reference lane:** romantic rap/R&B duet, soul-sample warmth, luxury tenderness, emotionally resolved hook

### Musical fingerprint
- Tempo/feel: slow-to-mid tempo, smooth, intimate, more R&B sway than hard rap.
- Drums: restrained, polished, soft knock; no aggressive trap clutter.
- Harmony: warm soul/R&B chords; sample-like romance and classic-soul glow.
- Vocals: male rap/sung intimacy plus female R&B lift; duet contrast matters.
- Hook: tender, memorable, repeated as emotional promise.
- Lyrics: devotion, protection, vulnerability, romance, legacy; elegant rather than crude.
- Mix: expensive, warm, close vocals, wide chorus harmonies.

### MiniMax prompt translation
A warm romantic rap/R&B duet with polished drums, soul-sample glow, close male rap-sung verses, and a soaring female R&B chorus. The song should feel intimate, mature, protective, and luxurious, with a simple emotional hook that sounds like a promise rather than a flex.

### Best AutoAlbum use case
Lead single, love-song centerpiece, emotional relief after a harder rap section, or album duet moment.

### Avoid / preserve distinction
Do not make it a diss record, club banger, or generic trap ballad. Preserve warmth, restraint, and duet chemistry.

## Kendrick Lamar — `Not Like Us` lane

**Reference lane:** West Coast diss anthem, Mustard bounce, chant hook, regional victory lap

### Musical fingerprint
- Tempo/feel: fast West Coast bounce; danceable and taunting.
- Drums: bright claps, hard snap, DJ/drop energy, minimal clutter.
- Bass: short sub hits with bounce rather than long trap slides.
- Melody: sparse, recognizable, slightly eerie but club-ready.
- Vocals: taunting, nasal emphasis, crowd-call delivery, high articulation.
- Hook: slogan chant; extremely repeatable; crowd can shout it after one listen.
- Lyrics: competitive, accusatory, comedic, region-proud, punchline-heavy.
- Arrangement: intro phrase/drop, verse-hook, repeated chant payoff.

### MiniMax prompt translation
A fast West Coast diss-anthem with bright claps, clipped bass hits, sparse eerie synths, and a taunting lead vocal. The hook should be a sharp crowd-chant slogan that lands immediately, with verses full of competitive punchlines, regional pride, and comedic bite.

### Best AutoAlbum use case
Battle track, antagonist track, album energy spike, regional anthem, or crowd-response single.

### Avoid / preserve distinction
Do not overcomplicate harmony. Do not make the hook melodic and soft. Preserve danceable menace and chant simplicity.
