# Current Rap Artist Fingerprint Precision Addendum

Use this addendum to refine strictness 4-5 artist prompts. The goal is non-confusion: a style card should not be able to pass as a neighboring artist.

## Kendrick Lamar vs J. Cole vs Drake
- Kendrick: theatrical character switches, rhythmic volatility, West Coast identity or cinematic moral pressure, chant/slogan hooks, voice as an instrument of argument.
- J. Cole: warm self-produced/jazz-soul humility, conversational narrative, introspective but less theatrical, traditional rap architecture.
- Drake: melody/caption/confessional lifestyle, smooth delivery, luxury/night-drive atmosphere, less syllabic pressure.

## Playboi Carti vs Yeat vs Ken Carson
- Carti: persona-first, ad-lib ecology, baby/deep voice contrasts, minimal lyric density, moshpit chant energy, beat as atmosphere.
- Yeat: alien slang, bell/synth motifs, woozy Auto-Tune, euphoric rage-pocket, more melodic mumble texture.
- Ken Carson: harsher Opium aggression, chaotic distortion, darker digital punk energy, more blunt repetitive hedonism.

## Travis Scott vs Don Toliver
- Travis: festival-scale psychedelic trap, stacked ad-libs, cavernous reverb, beat-switch spectacle, vocal as texture.
- Don Toliver: elastic melodic lead, smoother romance/night-drive luxury, cleaner hook glide, less chaotic ad-lib architecture.

## Future vs Gunna vs Lil Baby
- Future: narcotic toxicity, melodic monotone, bleak luxury, repetition as hypnosis, 808-heavy emotional numbness.
- Gunna: smooth flow, fashion/luxury float, cleaner pocket, less desperation, gliding through beat rather than attacking it.
- Lil Baby: urgent breathless street-melodic flow, long phrase chains, moral pressure, forward momentum.

## GloRilla vs Sexyy Red
- GloRilla: Memphis stomp, raspy command, motivational aggression, gang/crew chant, heavy drums.
- Sexyy Red: raunchy minimalist chant, deadpan humor, blunt repetition, party function, intentionally simple structure.

## Central Cee vs Lil Baby vs Drake
- Central Cee: UK drill/rap articulation, dry voice, compact punchlines, cold restraint, UK slang and pocket.
- Lil Baby: Atlanta street urgency, melodic runs, dense breathless flow.
- Drake: smoother melodic charisma, R&B crossover, less drill snap.

## 21 Savage vs Pooh Shiesty
- 21 Savage: deadpan menace, pocket discipline, negative space, cold one-liners, horror-minimal Atlanta trap.
- Pooh Shiesty: Memphis street menace, clipped delivery, harsher street immediacy, sparse ominous keys.

## Nicki Minaj vs Doechii vs Cardi B
- Nicki: theatrical voices, punchline gymnastics, pop-rap agility, character shifts.
- Doechii: elastic technicality, performance-art confidence, genre agility, sharp character sketches.
- Cardi: brash personality, direct charisma, club/Latin/NY energy, quote-first delivery.

## Strictness 5 rule
For strictness 5, every prompt must include at least three artist-distinguishing controls and two adjacent-artist avoid controls.
