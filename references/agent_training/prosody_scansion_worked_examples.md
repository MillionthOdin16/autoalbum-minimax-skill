# Prosody, Scansion, and Topline Worked Examples

Use this file when judging whether lyrics can be sung or rapped. Do not pass a lyric merely because the meaning is strong.

## Basic method

For each important section:

1. Count rough syllables per line.
2. Mark natural stresses.
3. Identify breath points.
4. Check whether the title/hook phrase lands on strong beats or memorable rhythmic positions.
5. Check whether line length matches the intended style.
6. Identify awkward consonant clusters, tongue-twisters, and overpacked lines.
7. Verify section contrast: verse can be denser; chorus should usually be simpler and more repeatable.

## Example: overpacked hook

Weak:

```text
[Chorus]
I am trying to reconcile the contradiction of wanting freedom and wanting you near
```

Problems:
- too many syllables
- abstract nouns
- no obvious melodic landing
- no repeatable title phrase
- breathless

Improved:

```text
[Chorus]
I want out
I want you near
Two locked doors
One room here
```

Why better:
- short lines
- repetition/contrast
- open vowel on “out” and “you”
- easier to place over melody
- tension is embodied, not explained

## Example: title stress

Title: “After the Fire”

Weak placement:

```text
I was walking after the fire in my head again
```

The title is buried mid-line and loses weight.

Better:

```text
After the fire
I counted what stayed
```

The title lands as a phrase with space after it.

## Example: rap cadence density

Flat rap verse:

```text
I got money I got problems I got people who been watching me
I keep moving I keep winning I keep doing what they doubted me
```

Problems:
- repetitive grammar
- weak internal rhyme
- no rests
- generic content

Improved:

```text
Money on mute, I let the phone light talk
New chain cold but the hallway stalks
They clap when I enter, they count when I leave
I learned what a smile looks like through teeth
```

Why better:
- stronger images
- cadence variation
- internal sound play
- paranoia theme with concrete details

## Example: R&B breath and negative space

Weak alt-R&B verse:

```text
I was thinking about the complicated nature of our silence when you did not call me back
```

Improved:

```text
You read it
Then the room got loud
I left my phone face-down
Like that would help somehow
```

Why better:
- conversational
- breath points
- concrete action
- intimate mood

## Hook phonetics

Good hook phrases often use:
- open vowels: ah, oh, ay, eye, oo
- repeated consonants that are easy to sing
- short words on strong beats
- a phrase the listener can repeat without reading lyrics

Hard hook phrases often include:
- too many multisyllabic abstract words
- heavy consonant clusters
- legal/academic phrasing
- long subordinate clauses

## Prosody scoring guide

9-10: lyrics practically imply a melody/flow; hook is easy to deliver; stresses and meaning align.
7-8: mostly performable; minor weak lines or dense spots.
5-6: readable but awkward to sing/rap; likely weak MiniMax output.
0-4: not song-ready; rewrite before prompt compilation.
