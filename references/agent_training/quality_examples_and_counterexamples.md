# Quality Examples and Counterexamples

Use this file to train judgment. The agent should compare outputs against these patterns.

## Generic vs controlled style prompt

Generic:
```text
A sad rap song with emotional lyrics and a hard beat.
```

Controlled:
```text
A slow melodic trap song with a half-time 808 bounce, sparse minor piano loop, close Auto-Tuned sung-rap vocals, and a chorus that turns a private confession into a simple chant. The verses should feel numb and conversational; the hook should widen with stacked vocal doubles while keeping the drums dark and restrained.
```

## Weak vs strong album thesis

Weak:
```text
This album is about heartbreak, fame, and finding yourself.
```

Strong:
```text
The album follows a narrator who realizes that fame has not made them visible; it has made every private room public. The sound moves from dry, claustrophobic trap minimalism into wider, more synthetic choruses, ending not in triumph but in controlled self-possession.
```

## Weak vs strong track function

Weak:
```text
Track 4 is a sad song about missing someone.
```

Strong:
```text
Track 4 is the first emotional collapse after the album's confident opening run. It drops tempo, removes most percussion until the hook, and uses a narrow vocal range to make the narrator sound cornered. It must create contrast before Track 5 reintroduces motion.
```

## Weak vs strong hook contract

Weak:
```text
The hook should be catchy and emotional.
```

Strong:
```text
The hook phrase is “everybody knows my room.” It should repeat twice per chorus, with the title phrase landing on long open vowels. The first chorus is small and paranoid; the final chorus adds stacked doubles and sub-bass so the line feels less like fear and more like accusation.
```

## Weak vs strong lyric revision

Weak line:
```text
I hide my pain behind the smile that everybody sees.
```

Problems:
- familiar phrasing
- abstract pain/smile contrast
- no concrete scene

Better:
```text
I practiced that grin in the elevator chrome.
```

Why better:
- specific object
- performance/fame implied
- more original
- singable phrase

## Weak vs strong candidate review

Weak:
```text
Candidate B is better. It sounds more professional.
```

Strong:
```text
Candidate B wins because the hook arrives at 00:46 with a clear vocal lift and the 808 pattern finally separates the chorus from the verse. Candidate A has cleaner vocals but no memorable chorus; Candidate C has the best atmosphere but loses lyric intelligibility at 01:12 and 01:47.
```

## Weak vs strong sequence note

Weak:
```text
Track 6 fits after Track 5 because both are dark.
```

Strong:
```text
Track 6 should not follow Track 5 because both use slow half-time trap drums, minor piano loops, and dry lead vocals. Move Track 6 after the brighter Track 7 or revise Track 6 with a percussion reset and different harmonic color.
```
