# Hook, Title, and Chorus Lab

Run this before full lyric drafting for every important song. The hook is not decoration; it is the song's memory engine.

## Hook types

1. **Title hook**: title phrase is repeated in chorus.
2. **Chant hook**: short rhythmic phrase or command.
3. **Melodic hook**: memorable sung contour even if lyric is simple.
4. **Post-chorus hook**: repeated phrase after chorus.
5. **Riff/production hook**: instrumental motif or sound design event.
6. **Cadence hook**: rap flow or rhythmic pocket becomes memorable.
7. **Concept hook**: unusual premise or image drives replay.

## Hook contract

Every track must define:

```text
Title / hook phrase:
Hook type:
Listener memory target:
Where hook first appears:
How chorus/hook differs from verse:
Why it fits the style:
Why it belongs on this album:
Risk if MiniMax renders it weakly:
Backup hook strategy:
```

## Chorus design rules

- Chorus usually needs fewer words than verses.
- The emotional thesis should be obvious without explaining the whole song.
- The title should appear early unless the style intentionally avoids it.
- Use repetition, but not filler.
- The chorus should either lift, drop, widen, become more intimate, or otherwise change energy.
- In rap, the hook may be chant-like, melodic, ad-lib-driven, or phrase-based.
- In R&B, the hook can be understated but still must be memorable.

## Strong chorus traits

- clear title or anchor phrase
- vowel-friendly phrase
- repeatable rhythm
- direct emotional payoff
- contrast from verse density
- easy to summarize

## Weak chorus traits

- same density as verse
- abstract thesis statement
- no title phrase
- too many clauses
- no sonic or rhythmic lift
- cannot be remembered after one read

## Hook lab process

1. Generate 8 title/hook candidates.
2. Reject candidates that are generic, hard to sing, or stylistically wrong.
3. Select top 3.
4. Write a 2-line chorus nucleus for each.
5. Score each for memorability, style fit, album fit, and MiniMax likelihood.
6. Keep one primary and one backup.

## Example candidates

Concept: fame causing paranoia.

Weak: “Famous and Alone” — too generic.
Better: “Everybody Knows My Room” — specific, eerie, title-worthy.
Better: “Smile for the Cameras” — direct, rhythmic, visual.
Better: “The Glass Keeps Calling” — poetic, but may need clearer chorus.

## Hook scoring

```text
memorability: 0-10
singability/rapability: 0-10
style fit: 0-10
album fit: 0-10
novelty: 0-10
MiniMax render likelihood: 0-10
```

Do not approve a song unless the selected hook averages 8+ for serious/release-grade work.
