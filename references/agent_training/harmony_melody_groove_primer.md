# Harmony, Melody, and Groove Primer

This reference gives enough music theory for a non-expert agent to make useful decisions without pretending to be a composer.

## Harmony: how chords color emotion

Do not only say “minor” or “major.” Describe the harmonic language.

### Common harmonic lanes

- **Minor trap loop**: dark 2-4 chord loop, sparse, bass-forward, often modal.
- **Pop major/minor contrast**: verse in darker color, chorus brightens or opens.
- **Alt-R&B extended harmony**: 7ths, 9ths, suspended chords, unresolved color, Rhodes/guitar/synth pads.
- **Gospel/soul color**: richer extensions, turnarounds, backing-vocal lift, emotional cadences.
- **Rock/punk power progression**: simple, forceful, guitar-driven; energy from performance not complexity.
- **Country diatonic clarity**: clear chords supporting story; occasional borrowed chord for lift.
- **Electronic modal loop**: hypnotic repetition with texture/filter/motion changes.

## Melody/topline: how the vocal moves

Useful melody descriptors:

- narrow-range intimate melody
- chant-like repeated pitch
- rising chorus contour
- falling resigned chorus
- call-and-response
- conversational half-sung melody
- falsetto lift
- monotone menace
- wide leap into hook
- clipped rhythmic rap cadence

For MiniMax prompts, say what the melody should feel like, not exact notes.

## Groove: how the song moves physically

### Groove descriptors

- straight 4/4
- swung R&B pocket
- half-time trap bounce
- double-time rap flow over slow beat
- four-on-the-floor house pulse
- dembow/tresillo reggaeton rhythm
- amapiano log-drum bounce
- drill sliding 808 feel
- boom-bap head-nod pocket
- pop-punk driving eighth notes
- laid-back country shuffle

## Bass behavior

Bass is a core style marker.

- **808 slides**: trap, drill, rage, modern rap.
- **Sub-bass pulse**: electronic/R&B/pop.
- **Live electric bass**: funk, soul, rock, disco, country.
- **Log drum**: amapiano.
- **Reese bass**: drum and bass, darker electronic.
- **Walking/root motion**: jazz/soul/country contexts.

## Drum behavior

- **808 hats/trap snares**: modern rap/trap.
- **Sparse rim/click percussion**: dark pop/alt-R&B.
- **Four-on-the-floor kick**: house/techno/dance-pop.
- **Breakbeat**: jungle/drum and bass/alt-electronic.
- **Live kit backbeat**: rock/country/pop-punk.
- **Dembow rhythm**: reggaeton/dancehall-derived styles.

## How to use this in prompts

Weak:
```text
Emotional dark song with good melody and beat.
```

Strong:
```text
A dark minor-mode melodic trap song with a slow half-time 808 bounce, sparse piano loop, sliding sub bass, and a narrow intimate sung-rap verse that opens into a simple rising hook.
```

## Non-expert guardrail

If the agent cannot name the groove, bass behavior, vocal delivery, and hook type, the musical design is incomplete.
