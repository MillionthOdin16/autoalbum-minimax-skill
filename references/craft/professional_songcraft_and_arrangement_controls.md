# Professional Songcraft and Arrangement Controls

## Purpose

Use this file to make AutoAlbum behave like a serious songwriter/producer rather than a generic prompt generator. These controls should influence album foundation, track briefs, lyrics, MiniMax prompts, variants, and post-generation review.

## Album-level craft

A professional album is not a playlist. It needs:

```text
identity: recognizable sound within 10 seconds
promise: first 1-3 tracks tell listeners what record they are in
contrast: tracks differ in tempo, density, emotion, and arrangement function
continuity: sonic DNA, motifs, vocal identity, or lyrical worldview persists
stakes: emotional or energetic progression across the sequence
payoff: closer resolves, reframes, or intentionally denies resolution
```

## Track-level contract

Every track must define:

```text
album function: opener / single / deep cut / interlude / climax / reset / closer
listener promise: what the track gives the listener immediately
memorable moment: hook, riff, chant, vocal gesture, beat switch, bridge, drop, or lyric turn
contrast mechanism: what changes vs previous track
continuity mechanism: what remains part of album DNA
```

If a track cannot answer these, cut or redesign it.

## Hook design

A hook must be:

```text
simple enough to remember
specific enough to belong to this song
performable in the intended vocal style
placed where the track needs payoff
aligned with title or title-adjacent language unless intentionally oblique
```

Rap hooks can be:

```text
chant hook
melodic hook
one-line slogan
call-response
post-chorus earworm
repeated ad-lib cell
sample/motif hook
```

Do not force a sung pop chorus into every rap track. Some rap songs work because the hook is rhythmic, phonetic, and physical.

## Melody and topline controls

Even when MiniMax invents the melody, the packet should imply topline behavior:

```text
narrow-range chant
falling confessional melody
rising pre-chorus lift
talk-rap verse with sung hook
floating Auto-Tuned phrase
staccato percussive rap
wide chorus harmonies
```

Prompt with behavior, not exact notes.

## Lyric craft

Strong album lyrics are:

```text
specific
singable
section-aware
emotionally legible
free of placeholder abstraction
connected to album thesis
not overstuffed with five ideas per verse
```

For rap, check:

```text
flow pocket
internal rhyme density
breathing room
punchline placement
ad-lib space
hook/title integration
verse two escalation
```

For melodic songs, check:

```text
open vowels in hook
line length
stress pattern
pre-chorus tension
chorus release
bridge contrast
```

## Harmony and music-theory control

Music theory should guide feeling, not create academic clutter. Use:

```text
minor-mode vs major-mode color
modal mixture / borrowed-chord color
pedal tones for tension
simpler loop for rap focus
richer extensions for R&B/neo-soul
chromatic movement for darkness
relative major/minor shift for bridge contrast
```

In MiniMax prompts, translate theory into audible language:

```text
"minor-mode piano loop with one brighter borrowed-chord lift in the chorus"
"warm extended R&B chords on Rhodes"
"dark two-chord synth loop that leaves room for the vocal"
```

## Groove controls

Groove is often more important than harmony for style fidelity.

Track prompts should specify:

```text
straight vs swung
half-time vs double-time feel
four-on-the-floor vs trap pattern
snare/clap placement
hat density
percussion character
bass/kick relationship
pocket: ahead / behind / relaxed / urgent
```

## Arrangement energy map

Use a 1-9 energy scale for every section.

Example:

```text
[Intro] 2
[Verse] 4
[Pre Chorus] 5
[Chorus] 8
[Verse] 5
[Bridge] 3
[Chorus] 9
[Outro] 2
```

The map should make the track breathe. Flat energy is a professional failure unless intentionally hypnotic.

## Bridge / break / interlude rules

A bridge or break must do one of:

```text
change harmony
change groove
change vocal perspective
remove drums
introduce silence/dropout
flip emotional meaning
prepare final chorus/drop
```

If the bridge is only extra words over the same loop, cut it.

## Production identity

Each album needs a production DNA statement:

```text
vocal placement
reverb world
drum philosophy
low-end philosophy
instrument palette
texture density
mix depth
sample/synth/guitar/piano preference
```

Each track needs a delta:

```text
more sparse
more aggressive
more melodic
more synthetic
more live-band
more intimate
more anthemic
```

## Sequence review after audio

Paper sequencing is provisional. After renders exist, evaluate:

```text
best opener after hearing audio
single placement
mid-album sag
tempo clustering
key/tonal fatigue
similar drum palettes
lyric mood repetition
closer impact
transitions and silence between tracks
```

Then resequence if needed.

## Professional rejection rule

If a render is merely okay, do not keep it because the packet was good. Keep only tracks that either:

```text
have clear replay value
serve the album arc indispensably
contain a standout performance/moment
unlock the next track in the sequence
```
